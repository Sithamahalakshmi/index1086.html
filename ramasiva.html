historical places in india

RED FORT

The Red Fort Complex was built as the palace fort of Shahjahanabad – the new capital of the fifth Mughal Emperor of India, Shah Jahan. Named for its massive enclosing walls of red sandstone, it is adjacent to an older fort, the Salimgarh, built by Islam Shah Suri in 1546

AYODHYA

Ayodhya is a city situated on the banks of the Sarayu river in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh. It is the administrative headquarters of the Ayodhya district as well as the Ayodhya division of Uttar Pradesh, India. Ayodhya was historically known as Saketa

GOLDEN TEMPLE

It is the pre-eminent spiritual site of Sikhism. It is one of the holiest sites in Sikhism, alongside the Gurdwara Darbar Sahib Kartarpur in Kartarpur, and Gurdwara Janam Asthan in Nankana Sahib.

LOTUS TEMPLE

The Lotus Temple, located in Delhi, India, is a Bahá'í House of Worship that was dedicated in December 1986, costing $10 million. Notable for its flowerlike shape, it has become a prominent attraction in the city.

AJANTA CAVES

These are a series of rock-cut caves on Waghora river near Aurangabad in Maharashtra. There are a total of 29 buddhist caves in Ajanta of which 4 were used as Chaitya or prayer halls while 25 were used as Viharas or residential caves..

GOLCONDA FORT

Golconda is a fortified citadel and ruined city located in the western outskirts of Hyderabad, Telangana, India. The fort was originally built by Kakatiya ruler Pratāparudra in the 11th century out of mud walls.

VICTORIA MEMORIAL

One of the landmarks of Kolkata, Victoria Memorial is a grand British-era built entirely of white marble. Constructed in Indo-Saracenic revivalist style, using a mix of British and Mughal the memorial draws influences from Islamic, Venetian, Egyptian and Deccani styles.

GATEWAY OF INDIA

The Gateway of India is an arch monument built during the 20th century in India. The monument erected to commemorate the landing of King GeorgV and Queen Mary at Apollo Bunder on their visit to India in 1911.
